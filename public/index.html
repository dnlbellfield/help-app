Where the guidance comes from

Repair is source-informed support, not therapy or a diagnosis. It combines practical skills from established clinical and relationship frameworks, then applies them to the situation you describe.

  • DBT skillsemotion regulation, distress tolerance, pausing before harmful action.
  • Gottman repair conceptsrepair attempts, de-escalation, returning after conflict.
  • The Hotlinehealthy vs. unhealthy vs. abusive relationship behavior, boundaries, safety.
  • 988 Lifelinecrisis support when someone may be unsafe or overwhelmed beyond app support.
  • Nonviolent Communicationobservation, feeling, need, request language for safer messages.
  • SAMHSA trauma-informed approachsafety, choice, collaboration, empowerment, and avoiding retraumatizing responses.

For repeated aggression, lying, emotional abuse, or fear of escalation, use Repair as a bridge to real support: individual therapy, DBT skills training, couples therapy when safe, or a local interpersonal violence intervention program.
