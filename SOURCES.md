# Repair Sources

Repair is source-informed educational support, not therapy, diagnosis, or crisis care.

The guidance is shaped by:

- DBT skills: emotion regulation, distress tolerance, pausing before harmful action.
  https://behavioraltech.org/resources/faqs/dialectical-behavior-therapy-dbt/
- Gottman repair concepts: repair attempts, de-escalation, and returning after conflict.
  https://www.gottman.com/blog/r-is-for-repair/
- The National Domestic Violence Hotline: relationship spectrum, boundaries, safety, abusive behavior warning signs, and resources for people causing harm.
  https://www.thehotline.org/resources/healthy-relationships/
- 988 Lifeline: crisis support when someone may be unsafe or overwhelmed beyond app support.
  https://988lifeline.org/
- Nonviolent Communication: observation, feeling, need, request language.
  https://www.cnvc.org/
- SAMHSA trauma-informed approach: safety, trust, choice, collaboration, empowerment, and resisting retraumatization.
  https://store.samhsa.gov/product/samhsas-concept-trauma-and-guidance-trauma-informed-approach/sma14-4884

For repeated aggression, lying, emotional abuse, or fear of escalation, Repair should point toward real support: individual therapy, DBT skills training, couples therapy when safe, or a local interpersonal violence intervention program.
